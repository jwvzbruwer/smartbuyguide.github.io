What to Consider When Buying an Affordable Gaming Monitor

Before diving into our top recommendations, it‚Äôs essential to understand the key features that make a monitor ideal for gaming. Here are some factors to consider:

Refresh Rate

The refresh rate, measured in hertz (Hz), determines how many times a monitor updates the image per second. For gaming, a higher refresh rate (like 144Hz or 240Hz) provides smoother gameplay. Look for monitors with at least a 60Hz refresh rate for casual gaming, but if you‚Äôre serious about competitive gaming, aim for 144Hz or higher.

Response Time

Response time refers to how quickly a pixel can change from one color to another, measured in milliseconds (ms). A lower response time (1ms to 5ms) is ideal for reducing motion blur and ghosting, which can be particularly noticeable in fast-paced games.

Resolution

Resolution affects the clarity of the image. Common resolutions include 1080p (Full HD), 1440p (Quad HD), and 4K (Ultra HD). While 4K monitors provide stunning visuals, they can be expensive. For affordability, a 1080p resolution is a great starting point, especially for gamers on a budget.

Panel Type

The type of panel also impacts performance and price. The three main types are:

  • IPS (In-Plane Switching): Offers the best color accuracy and viewing angles, but can be pricier.
  • TN (Twisted Nematic): Generally the most affordable, with fast response times but poorer color reproduction.
  • VA (Vertical Alignment): Good contrast ratios and decent color accuracy, often falling in the mid-range price.
